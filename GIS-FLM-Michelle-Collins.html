Your Top 3 Recommended Actions

Personalized
1 Keep Stack-Ranking Priorities Explicitly and Often Continue & Amplify

Your team called out one specific moment — when you told them ACT courses weren't the top priority right now — as incredibly impactful. That kind of explicit, honest signaling cuts through noise in a way that general guidance doesn't. With GTM strategy, AI tooling, and mandatory training all competing for attention, this is your highest-leverage habit.

Action: Make priority stack-ranking a standing part of your team communications. Even a brief weekly signal — "this week, focus on X over Y" — would have outsized impact. When leadership pushes competing priorities, translate those into a ranked order for your team rather than passing along everything at once.

2 Close the AI Adoption Gap at the Individual Level 30 Days

Despite strong overall AI sentiment on your team, some members still aren't using Claude or Cursor despite multiple enablements. Group sessions aren't landing for everyone — the gap needs to be closed one person at a time.

Action: In your next round of 1:1s, ask each SE directly: "What's your current Claude/Cursor usage like, and what's getting in the way?" For those who are behind, arrange a peer pairing session with a team member who uses it well, rather than sending them to another group training.

3 Escalate Headcount With Survey Data Behind You 30 Days

Your team knows you're advocating for them — and that matters. But the ask for more headcount is consistent and urgent. Your team's strong scores give you a credible platform: you can make the case that investing in this high-performing team is a high-return decision.

Action: Build a brief business case for headcount: account load, SE ratios, LOA coverage gaps heading into summer, and the retention risk if workload stays unsustainable. Present it with the GIS data as evidence. Your results make this advocacy more likely to land than it would from a leader with struggling scores.
