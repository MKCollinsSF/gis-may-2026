Your Top 3 Recommended Actions

Personalized
1 Establish a Structured Goal-Setting Process 30 Days

With a 71% 12-month foresee score and limited open-text signal explaining the "why," the highest-leverage starting point is building clear, documented quarterly goals for each SE so they can see their own trajectory. When people can see where they're headed and how they're tracking, uncertainty decreases.

Action: Before the end of Q3, sit down with each SE to co-create written goals covering business outcomes and personal development. Build a monthly check-in on those goals into your 1:1 cadence. This creates a feedback loop and gives you much richer data on what's driving scores by next cycle.

2 Make Career Conversations a Standing 1:1 Agenda Item 30 Days

The 71% 12-month foresee score alongside an 88% TMI suggests some people trust you and value working with you, but aren't certain about their future here. Career conversations Ś done consistently, not just at performance review time Ś are the fastest way to surface and address that uncertainty before it becomes a departure decision.

Action: Add "career development" as a standing agenda item in monthly 1:1s. Ask directly: "What does success look like for you in the next 12 months, and how can I help you get there?" Listen more than you advise. Make it a consistent practice, not a one-time check-in.

3 Run a Direct Team Conversation to Understand the Scores Next Team Meeting

Limited open-text feedback means the quantitative scores are telling part of the story but not all of it. A candid, direct team conversation will surface more in 30 minutes than the survey data can on its own Ś and it signals that you take the results seriously and want to act on them.

Action: In your next team meeting, share the key scores openly and ask two questions: "What would make this a 10 out of 10 place to work for you?" and "What's the one thing I could do differently that would make the biggest difference?" Capture the answers and follow up with what you're doing about them. That follow-through is what builds trust for the next cycle.
