Your Top 3 Recommended Actions

Personalized
1 Treat the Stay Rate as an Emergency — Escalate With Data Urgent

A 44% stay rate (‑22 pts) with LOAs incoming is a ticking clock. Your team knows you're advocating for them and trusts you deeply — but they need to see structural relief, not just advocacy. You need to make the math impossible for leadership to ignore.

Action: Build a one-page brief: 9 SEs, X active accounts, Y LOAs starting [date], Z ratio of SEs to work if no action is taken. Pair it with the GIS retention data. Present it to leadership as an emergency requiring a decision, not a discussion. Set a clear ask: what specifically will change by what date?

2 Drive AI Tool Adoption From Aspiration to Accountability 30 Days

Your team explicitly asked for 100% AI tool adoption and weekly Org26 dashboards to track it. The org's AI confidence score (80%) is below goal — your team has a real opportunity to close that gap and demonstrate leadership in this area.

Action: Set an explicit team goal for AI tool adoption with a target date. Create a lightweight weekly team dashboard tracking Org26 metrics. In 1:1s, ask each SE specifically: "What AI tool are you using most, and what's one thing you want to try next week?" Make it a normal part of the work conversation.

3 Push for DSR Model Accountability Clarification 60 Days

The DSR model frustration is most acute in your team's data. SEs feel they are the "quarterback" carrying coordination weight that belongs to others in the model — and that the model creates work without creating accountability for non-SE team members.

Action: Work with leadership to establish clear, documented accountability rules for the DSR model — specifically what Account SEs own vs. what extended team members are accountable for. Bring specific examples of where the model is breaking down. This protects your team's time and reduces burnout from the coordination overhead.
