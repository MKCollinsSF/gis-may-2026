Your Top 3 Recommended Actions

Personalized
1 Share Your Playbook With Peer Leaders High Impact

Perfect scores across every dimension make your approach the model the org needs to learn from. What you're doing in 1:1s, how you build psychological safety, how you communicate — these are practices other leaders need but may not have language for yet.

Action: Work with leadership to document and share your approach — whether through a peer leader session, a written reflection, or informal coaching conversations with other FLMs. You don't need to present it as a formula; sharing how you think about specific situations is enough.

2 Help Your Team Define What to Push Back On 30 Days

Even with outstanding engagement, the team asked for clearer guidance on which work is outside SE scope. This is a skill — knowing when and how to say no — that protects your team's focus and reduces burnout long-term.

Action: In your next team meeting or 1:1s, explicitly name 2–3 categories of work that are outside SE scope and give the team language to decline or redirect those requests. Make this a repeatable conversation, not a one-time answer.

3 Use Your Credibility to Advocate for IC Career Pathing 60 Days

The org-wide ceiling on senior IC advancement is a systemic retention risk that will eventually affect your team too — even with perfect satisfaction scores today. You are well-positioned to push for change at the leadership level because your results give your voice significant weight.

Action: Raise the IC career pathing issue in leadership forums with specific proposals — new levels, a leadership track, or visible stretch opportunities. Frame it as a retention investment for the org, not just your team. Your credibility makes this advocacy more likely to land.
