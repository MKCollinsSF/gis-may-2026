Your Top 3 Recommended Actions

Personalized
1 Document and Share What Changed Since November High Impact

A +18 engagement and +14 stay rate improvement in six months is exceptional — the strongest turnaround in the org. The organization needs to understand what specifically drove this so other leaders can learn from it.

Action: Reflect on and articulate the 2–3 things you did differently since November. Was it communication cadence, deal involvement, how you ran 1:1s, how you handled a difficult situation? Write it down and share it with leadership as a model for the broader FLM group.

2 Create a Regular AI Knowledge-Sharing Forum 30 Days

Your team is explicitly asking for more organized AI knowledge sharing — prompts, use cases, and workflows. The org-wide AI confidence score (80%) is below the 85% goal, and your team can help close that gap for the broader group too.

Action: Set up a lightweight async Slack channel or monthly 30-minute team session dedicated to AI sharing. Seed it yourself with one use case per week to establish the norm. Invite your team members to contribute their own. Consider opening it to adjacent teams to drive the cross-team collaboration your team is also asking for.

3 Drive Cross-Team Collaboration 60 Days

Your team specifically asked for more cross-collaboration with Core SEs from other RCG SE teams. Given your strong engagement scores, connecting your team with others would help spread that energy and build broader cohesion across the org.

Action: Identify 1–2 joint working sessions or shared initiatives per quarter where your team collaborates with another FLM's team on a real problem — AI enablement, deal strategy, or a customer challenge. Make it work-based, not social-only.
