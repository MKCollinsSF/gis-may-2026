Your Top 3 Recommended Actions

Personalized
1 Have Direct Retention Conversations Now Urgent

A 33% stay rate (‑29 pts since November) is the most acute retention risk in the org. The good news: your TMI score shows the team values and trusts you. The decline is structural — burnout, unclear growth paths, and a newer team finding its footing — not a reflection of your relationship with them.

Action: Schedule 1:1 retention conversations with each team member in the next 30 days. Ask specifically: "What would make you want to stay here for the next two years?" and "What's the one thing that's most frustrating right now?" Listen, don't fix. Then escalate what's in leadership's control.

2 Cut Internal Noise — Protect Customer-Facing Time 30 Days

Your team described you as great in front of customers and in deals — but noted you get pulled into too many internal programs and processes. Your team is asking for the same protection for themselves. Reducing internal friction is one of the highest-leverage moves available given the deep work and meeting scores org-wide.

Action: Audit your team's recurring internal meetings. Cancel or make async anything that doesn't require a live decision. Push back on internal program asks that pull SEs away from accounts. Set an explicit norm: customer-facing work takes priority over internal requests.

3 Build Team Cohesion Around Real Work 60 Days

Multiple comments reflect that this is a newer team that hasn't had enough time to build relationships. The team explicitly said virtual attempts haven't worked well because customer work always interferes — and they don't want manufactured "team atmosphere" events. The ask is for connection through meaningful shared work.

Action: Create 2–3 structured moments this quarter where the team works together on something real — a deal strategy session, an AI tool demo challenge, or a shared account problem. Build cohesion as a byproduct of doing great work together, not as a standalone agenda item.
