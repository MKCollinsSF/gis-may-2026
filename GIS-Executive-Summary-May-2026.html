Additional Leadership Callouts

Career Pathing for Senior ICs
Multiple respondents flagged zero promotion levels above their current IC role � one noted 7 years without advancement opportunity. This is a compounding flight risk as AI-native competitors (OpenAI, Anthropic, Sierra) actively recruit from Salesforce.
Headcount & DSR Model Strain
SEs have been spread thin for 2+ consecutive years. The DSR model adds coordination overhead without clear accountability. LOA coverage is stretching the team heading into H2. Employees want visible headcount commitments, not promises.
GIS Follow-Through Perception Drop
"My manager took action on the most recent GIS results" dropped ‑13 pts since Nov 25 to 77%. Leadership must close the loop with visible, named actions tied to survey findings � or credibility will continue to erode with this team.
AI Enablement Fragmentation
AI initiatives are launching independently across teams, producing overlapping and misaligned training. Employees want a centralized, prioritized AI learning path � not 16 hours of mandatory training dropped in the final month of the quarter at quarter-end.

Recommended Actions

Top 5 Priorities

The following five actions are recommended to drive meaningful, measurable change before the next GIS cycle. They are sequenced by immediacy � #1 and #3 can begin within 30 days; #2 and #4 require 60�90 days to stand up properly; #5 enables and tracks all of the above.

1 Launch a Structured "Focus Time" Initiative Immediate

The 16% favorable score on uninterrupted deep work is the worst number in the entire survey � and it's the root cause feeding the bottom scores on meeting effectiveness and bureaucracy simultaneously, as well as burnout. One intervention addresses three bottom scores.

Actions: Designate org-wide no-internal-meeting blocks (e.g., Tuesday and Thursday mornings) enforced at the leader level. Shift recurring account syncs to async Slack updates as the default, with live meetings as the exception. Give managers explicit air cover to decline or cancel meetings that lack a clear agenda and decision to be made.

2 Create Visible Career Advancement Pathways for Senior ICs 60�90 Days

Career pathing for senior ICs is the retention risk that headcount alone won't fix. Open-text responses include one respondent noting 7 years in role without a promotion opportunity. With AI-native competitors (OpenAI, Anthropic, Sierra) actively recruiting, the window to act is now � team discretionary effort remains high (93%), meaning people haven't checked out yet.

Actions: Define and document what advancement looks like for senior SEs � new IC levels, a formal leadership track, or high-visibility project ownership that creates differentiated opportunity. Equip managers with a concrete framework for growth conversations. Identify 2�3 senior ICs who are flight risks and initiate direct conversations before the next cycle.

3 Communicate an Honest H2 Resourcing Plan Before LOA Season Hits Immediate

Headcount appears in nearly every open-text category. It is the single most consistent theme across the entire dataset. Employees don't necessarily need more headcount tomorrow � they need to know the plan is real. Silence while people assume the worst is the fastest path to accelerated attrition heading into H2.

Actions: Communicate a specific, honest H2 headcount plan � including what has been decided and what hasn't. Address LOA gap coverage proactively before summer starts. Clarify DSR model accountability so SEs understand what they own and what is owned by others.

4 Establish a Career Coaching Cadence with Structured Goal Tracking 60�90 Days

Manager relationships are a clear strength (TMI 95%), but employees lack clarity on where they're going and how they're tracking. Goal clarity and strategic alignment was the second most cited theme in "what could your manager do to improve team effectiveness." The foundation of trust is already there � the missing piece is structure.

Actions: Require written, co-created goals at the start of each quarter covering both business outcomes and individual development. Add a standing goal-tracking agenda item to monthly 1:1s � not just pipeline, but growth markers. Equip managers with a lightweight framework for quarterly reflection check-ins that connect daily work to career trajectory. Ensure performance ratings feel consistent with the conversations that preceded them.

5 Form a GIS Action Committee to Own Execution and Progress Tracking Enabler

Recommendations without accountability structures don't survive the quarter. This is the operating system for the other four actions. GIS follow-through perception dropped 13 points since November � the team has already experienced a cycle where candid feedback didn't produce visible change. A committee signals that this time is different.

Actions: Form a group of 5�7 people � a mix of FLMs and senior ICs, including voices from teams where scores are struggling. Assign a committee lead to each of the four action items above with 30/60/90 day milestones. Hold monthly working sessions with a quarterly all-hands update to the broader team. Agree in advance on outcome-based success metrics tied to specific GIS questions so the next survey cycle measures the delta directly. Create a lightweight ongoing feedback channel so SEs can flag whether changes are actually landing.
